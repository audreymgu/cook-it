chinese stir-fry rice cakes

(炒年糕)

A traditional Chinese dish, typically consumed to celebrate the new lunar year.

Time: 1 hour

ingredients

    primary
  • rice cakes
  • pork
  • napa cabbage

    • stocks and oils
  • chicken broth
  • cooking oil
  • cooking wine

    • seasonings
  • soy sauce
  • sesame seed oil
  • white pepper
  • sugar

preparation

  1. Soak rice cakes in a large saucepan. Ensure that cakes have been separated.
  2. Combine soy sauce, sesame seed oil, white pepper, and sugar in a medium-sized bowl. Stir well.
  3. Slice pork into small, narrow strips, approximately an inch or so in length. Mix in with seasoning and leave to marinate.
  4. Separate cabbage leaves and slice width-wise into thin strips. Set aside in a large bowl.
  5. Heat a large wok or saucepan on high heat and coat in oil. Add in pork and cook until almost completely browned.
  6. Stir in cabbage and a small amount of broth. Reduce heat to medium-high and cook for 2 to 3 minutes.
  7. Add in drained rice cakes and toss vigorously. Add broth gradually until both cabbage and rice cakes have started to soften.
  8. Sample rice cakes for chewiness and flavor. Cook for additional time and season to taste as needed. Serve immidiately.
